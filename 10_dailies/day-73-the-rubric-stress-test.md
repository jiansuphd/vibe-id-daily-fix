# Day 73: The 'Rubric Stress-Test'

- **The Pointer:** Stress-test a rubric by applying it to three synthetic student submissions — one exemplary, one borderline, one failing — and check for rater inconsistency.
- **The Details:** Rubrics break at the edges. The stress test reveals where criteria language is ambiguous (two raters would score differently) and forces clarification before the first real submission.
- **Action:**

```bash
gemini "Apply this rubric to three simulated submissions: [exemplary], [borderline], [failing]. Identify any criterion where your score feels arbitrary. Suggest clearer language."
```
